# Morocco 2BAC English Exam Prep - Printable Study Guide

This is an original, exam-focused guide for Moroccan 2nd Year Baccalaureate English revision. It covers English only.

Official exam details should always be checked with the latest Ministry or CNE documents.

## 1. Exam Structure

### Reading Comprehension

Expect one text about a familiar Bac theme such as youth, education, technology, citizenship, culture, environment, migration, or human rights.

Common tasks:

- True / false with justification
- Wh-questions
- Main idea
- Vocabulary from context
- Reference words
- Sentence completion
- Matching
- Inference
- Author attitude

Best method:

1. Read the title and first sentence.
2. Read the questions before reading deeply.
3. Underline keywords.
4. Answer from the text, not from your opinion.
5. For true or false, copy only a short proof.

### Language Use

Expect grammar, vocabulary, word formation, functions, sentence completion, and transformation.

Revise:

- Tenses
- Gerund and infinitive
- Modals
- Passive voice
- Reported speech
- Conditionals
- Relative clauses
- Connectors
- Phrasal verbs
- Articles
- Comparatives and superlatives
- Word formation
- Prefixes and suffixes

### Writing

Expect a paragraph, essay, email, letter, article, report, review, opinion essay, description, or argumentative text.

Good writing needs:

- A clear answer to the question
- Organized paragraphs
- Simple correct sentences
- Examples
- Connectors
- Final checking

## 2. Core Themes

### Gifts of Youth

Young people have energy, creativity, talents, and dreams. They also face challenges such as unemployment, peer pressure, stress, and lack of opportunities.

Key words: potential, ambition, challenge, creativity, self-confidence, peer pressure.

Useful sentences:

- Young people can make a difference in society.
- Volunteering helps students build confidence.
- Youth need guidance and opportunities.

Possible writing topics:

- How can young people help their community?
- Challenges facing Moroccan youth.
- Describe a young person who inspires you.

### Humour

Humour includes jokes, laughter, comedy, and satire. It can reduce stress, improve relationships, and criticize social problems politely.

Key words: laughter, comedian, sense of humour, satire, amusing, stress relief.

Useful sentences:

- Laughter is good for health.
- Humour can send a serious message.
- A good joke should not hurt people.

### Education

Formal education happens in schools. Informal education happens through family, media, and life experience. Non-formal education includes organized courses outside normal school.

Key words: literacy, scholarship, dropout, lifelong learning, e-learning, equal access.

Useful sentences:

- Education opens doors.
- Learning does not stop at school.
- Online learning can help students in remote areas.

### Cultural Issues and Values

Culture includes traditions, customs, identity, beliefs, and values. Respecting diversity helps people live together peacefully.

Key words: custom, tradition, identity, stereotype, diversity, tolerance.

Useful sentences:

- Culture shapes our identity.
- Diversity enriches society.
- Stereotypes can create misunderstanding.

### Women and Power

This theme focuses on women's rights, education, equality, leadership, and empowerment.

Key words: empowerment, equality, leadership, discrimination, achievement, role model.

Useful sentences:

- Educating girls benefits the whole society.
- Women can be leaders in every field.
- Equality means fair chances for all.

### Science and Technology

Technology can improve learning, communication, medicine, and daily life, but it can also cause addiction, privacy problems, and distraction.

Key words: innovation, device, artificial intelligence, digital, privacy, addiction.

Useful sentences:

- Technology is a tool, not a goal.
- Students should use the internet wisely.
- Innovation can improve people's lives.

### Sustainable Development

Sustainable development means improving life today without harming future generations.

Key words: sustainability, pollution, renewable energy, recycling, climate change, awareness.

Useful sentences:

- Small actions can protect the planet.
- Environmental awareness begins at school.
- Saving water is important in many Moroccan cities.

### Citizenship

Citizenship means respecting rights and duties, helping the community, protecting public property, and acting responsibly.

Key words: citizen, right, duty, volunteering, responsibility, public property.

Useful sentences:

- Good citizens respect both rights and duties.
- Volunteering teaches responsibility.
- Public property belongs to everyone.

### International Organizations

International organizations help solve global problems such as poverty, health, education, peace, and refugees.

Key words: organization, humanitarian, aid, peacekeeping, refugee, campaign.

Useful sentences:

- Global problems need global cooperation.
- Humanitarian work saves lives.
- Organizations can support vulnerable people.

### Brain Drain and Migration

Brain drain happens when skilled people leave their country for better opportunities. Migration can bring benefits and difficulties.

Key words: skilled worker, emigrate, shortage, investment, migrant, integration, remittances.

Useful sentences:

- Brain drain can weaken local development.
- Migration can bring both opportunities and difficulties.
- Countries should create opportunities for talent.

### Human Rights

Human rights are basic rights that every person should have, such as dignity, education, safety, equality, and freedom.

Key words: dignity, freedom, justice, equality, protection, violation.

Useful sentences:

- Human rights belong to everyone.
- A fair society protects vulnerable people.
- Rights come with responsibilities.

### Media and Communication

Media can educate, inform, entertain, and influence people. Students should avoid fake news and check sources.

Key words: media, communication, fake news, source, advertising, influence.

Useful sentences:

- Responsible users check information before sharing it.
- Media can educate and mislead at the same time.
- Good communication prevents misunderstanding.

## 3. Grammar Quick Summary

### Tenses

- Simple present: habits and facts. Example: Students revise every week.
- Present continuous: actions now. Example: She is preparing for the exam.
- Simple past: finished past. Example: They visited the library yesterday.
- Present perfect: experience or recent result. Example: He has finished his report.
- Past perfect: action before another past action. Example: They had left before I arrived.

### Gerund and Infinitive

- enjoy, avoid, mind + verb-ing
- want, decide, hope, agree + to + verb
- after prepositions + verb-ing

Examples:

- She enjoys reading.
- He decided to study.
- They are interested in volunteering.

### Modals

- Obligation: must, have to
- Advice: should, ought to
- Possibility: may, might, could
- Prohibition: must not
- Past advice/regret: should have + past participle

### Passive Voice

Form: be + past participle.

Examples:

- Trees were planted by students.
- English is spoken around the world.
- The report will be submitted tomorrow.

### Reported Speech

Change tense, pronouns, and time expressions when needed.

Examples:

- He said, "I am tired." -> He said that he was tired.
- She said, "I have finished." -> She said that she had finished.
- The teacher said, "Revise." -> The teacher told us to revise.

### Conditionals

- Type 0: If + present, present. If you heat water, it boils.
- Type 1: If + present, will. If I revise, I will pass.
- Type 2: If + past, would. If I had time, I would read more.
- Type 3: If + past perfect, would have + past participle. If she had revised, she would have passed.

### Relative Clauses

- who: people
- which: things
- where: places
- when: time
- whose: possession

### Connectors

- Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition
- Contrast: however, although, on the other hand
- Cause: because, since, due to
- Effect: therefore, as a result, consequently
- Example: for example, for instance
- Conclusion: in conclusion, to sum up

### Phrasal Verbs

Common examples:

- give up: stop trying
- look up: search for information
- look after: take care of
- find out: discover
- take part in: participate

### Word Formation

Check the sentence:

- noun: education, development, communication
- adjective: educational, successful, responsible
- adverb: clearly, responsibly, fluently
- prefix: unhappy, impossible, disagree
- suffix: useful, careless, development

## 5. Writing Formats

### Paragraph

Structure: topic sentence, supporting ideas, example, conclusion.

Checklist:

- One clear idea
- Connectors
- Example
- Final sentence

### Essay

Structure: introduction, body paragraph 1, body paragraph 2, conclusion.

Use: nowadays, first, moreover, however, in conclusion.

### Formal Letter

Use:

- Dear Sir or Madam,
- I am writing to ...
- I would like to ...
- I look forward to your reply.
- Yours faithfully,

### Informal Letter

Use:

- Dear friend,
- How are you?
- I am writing to tell you about ...
- Write back soon.
- Best wishes,

### Email

Be short and clear. Use a suitable subject if required.

### Article

Use a title, introduction, main ideas, and recommendation.

### Report

Use a title, aim, findings, and recommendations.

### Review

Mention the title, summary, opinion, and recommendation.

### Opinion Essay

State your opinion clearly, support it with reasons and examples, then conclude.

### Argumentative Writing

Present both sides, use contrast connectors, and state your final position.

## 6. Reading Strategy

### True / False With Justification

Write true or false, then give short proof from the text.

### Wh-Questions

Answer exactly what is asked. Do not copy a long paragraph.

### Main Idea

Choose the idea that covers the whole text.

### Vocabulary From Context

Look before and after the word.

### Reference Words

Find what it, they, them, this, that, who, or which refers to.

### Inference

Use clues in the text. Do not add your personal opinion.

### Author Attitude

Look for positive, negative, critical, neutral, or hopeful words.

## 7. High-Frequency Connectors

- Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition, also
- Contrast: however, although, whereas, on the other hand
- Cause: because, since, as, due to
- Effect: therefore, as a result, consequently, so
- Example: for example, for instance, such as
- Opinion: in my opinion, I believe that, from my point of view
- Conclusion: in conclusion, to sum up, overall

## 8. 7-Day Revision Plan

1. Review exam structure, reading strategies, Youth, and Education.
2. Review tenses, gerund and infinitive, modals, Culture, and Humour.
3. Review passive voice, reported speech, and common functions.
4. Review conditionals, relative clauses, connectors, Technology, Environment, and Citizenship.
5. Review writing formats and write one timed answer.
6. Complete one mock practice set and correct mistakes.
7. Light revision: vocabulary, connectors, writing phrases, and checklist.
